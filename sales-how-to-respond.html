Sales Tips – How to Respond

Practical wording and responses that keep conversations calm, clear, and professional.

Tip 1

Confidence-Setting Openers

(Establishes control immediately)

“Let’s have a look at what you’ve got.”

“We’ll sort this out step by step.”

“That’s a common one — we’ll get you the right match.”

“I see these pretty regularly.”

Why it works:

You’re not claiming mastery — you’re claiming process.

Tip 2

Buying Time Without Sounding Unsure

(When you need to think, measure, or check)

“Let me just double-check the sizing on this.”

“I want to make sure we don’t miss anything.”

“I’ll confirm that before we lock it in.”

“I’ve seen similar — just want to be certain.”

Avoid saying:

❌ “I’m not sure”

❌ “I don’t know much about these”

Tip 3

Redirecting Without Saying “You’re Wrong”

(Keeps customer confidence intact)

“Sometimes they’re referred to that way — let’s confirm it.”

“That term gets used for a few different things.”

“Let’s just line it up with what you’ve got here.”

“That’s close — the detail that matters is here.”

Why it works:

You validate the customer without agreeing blindly.

Tip 4

Measurement-First Language

(Positions accuracy as professionalism)

“If we match the measurements, the rest usually falls into place.”

“Dimensions will tell us more than the name.”

“Once we confirm ID and OD, it narrows fast.”

“Measurements take the guesswork out.”

This pairs perfectly with your bearing / seal / belt guides.

Tip 5

Handling “I Need It Now” Pressure

(Maintains authority without escalation)

“I’d rather take 30 seconds now than have you redo it later.”

“The right part saves more time than a quick guess.”

“Let’s get it right the first time.”

“This is one of those where accuracy matters.”

Tip 6

When You Know It’s the Wrong Part

(Without challenging ego)

“That would fit, but it won’t last in that application.”

“That option works in some cases — not this one.”

“I’ve seen those fail in setups like yours.”

“This is where the spec matters.”

Avoid:

❌ “That’s wrong”

❌ “You can’t use that”

Tip 7

When You Need to Escalate

(Without looking inexperienced)

“I want a second set of eyes on this.”

“This one’s worth confirming before we send you off.”

“Let me loop someone in so we get it spot on.”

“This falls into the ‘don’t guess’ category.”

This frames escalation as quality control, not lack of knowledge.

Tip 8

Closing Language That Feels Calm and Certain

(No pressure, no hype)

“This is the correct match for what you’ve described.”

“This will suit the application you mentioned.”

“That should solve the issue you’re seeing.”

“This is the safest option for that setup.”

Tip 9

If the Customer Challenges You

(Stay grounded, not defensive)

“Happy to walk through the reasoning if you like.”

“Let’s compare it to the one you had.”

“We’re aiming for reliability here.”

“I’m comfortable with this recommendation.”

Tip 10

Universal Confidence Reset

(Works in almost every scenario)

“Let’s slow it down and get it right.”

“We’ll base it on what matters.”

“We’ll go off facts, not guesses.”

“We’ll match it to the application.”
