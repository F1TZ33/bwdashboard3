Sales Tips – How We Add Value

Language choices that build trust, reinforce process, and show value without sounding uncertain.

Tip 1

Phrases That Undermine Confidence

(Even when the info is correct)

❌ “I’m not really sure…”

❌ “I think this is right…”

❌ “I don’t know much about these…”

❌ “I’ve never seen one of these before…”

Why to avoid:

They instantly hand control to the customer and invite pressure or dismissal.

Say instead:

✔ “Let’s check this properly.”

✔ “We’ll confirm the details.”

✔ “I want to make sure this matches.”

Tip 2

Phrases That Invite Guessing

(And future returns)

❌ “It should be fine.”

❌ “That’ll probably work.”

❌ “People usually use this.”

❌ “This is close enough.”

Why to avoid:

They sound casual — and casual parts fail.

Say instead:

✔ “This matches the dimensions.”

✔ “This suits that application.”

✔ “This is the correct type for that setup.”

Tip 3

Phrases That Challenge the Customer

(Creates resistance and ego conflict)

❌ “That’s wrong.”

❌ “You can’t use that.”

❌ “Who told you that?”

❌ “That’s not how it works.”

Why to avoid:

Even if true, they trigger defensiveness.

Say instead:

✔ “That term gets used a few different ways.”

✔ “Let’s line it up with what you’ve got.”

✔ “That works in some cases — not this one.”

Tip 4

Phrases That Over-Promise Expertise

(Risky and unnecessary)

❌ “I know exactly what you need.”

❌ “Trust me.”

❌ “I’ve been doing this for years.”

❌ “This is definitely the best option.”

Why to avoid:

If anything goes wrong, these come back hard.

Say instead:

✔ “Based on what you’ve described…”

✔ “This is the correct match for that setup.”

✔ “This aligns with the application.”

Tip 5

Phrases That Sound Defensive

(Weakens authority instantly)

❌ “That’s just what the book says.”

❌ “That’s how we always do it.”

❌ “I’m just following policy.”

❌ “Don’t blame me if…”

Why to avoid:

They make you sound powerless.

Say instead:

✔ “That’s the spec for this application.”

✔ “That’s what prevents repeat failures.”

✔ “That’s the safest option here.”

Tip 6

Phrases That Invite Rushing

(Pressure leads to mistakes)

❌ “If you’re in a hurry…”

❌ “We can just grab something quick.”

❌ “This should get you by.”

Why to avoid:

They signal corners are being cut.

Say instead:

✔ “Let’s take 30 seconds and confirm it.”

✔ “The right part saves time later.”

✔ “Accuracy matters here.”

Tip 7

Phrases That Give Away Control

(Especially with confident customers)

❌ “What do you want to do?”

❌ “Up to you.”

❌ “You tell me.”

❌ “Whatever you think.”

Why to avoid:

You’re the guide — not the passenger.

Say instead:

✔ “Here’s what I recommend.”

✔ “This is the correct path for that issue.”

✔ “This option avoids the common problems.”

Tip 8

Phrases That Devalue Measurement

(Dangerous in bearings, seals, belts, chain)

❌ “That looks about right.”

❌ “They’re usually all the same.”

❌ “It should fit.”

Why to avoid:

Dimensions are everything in mechanical parts.

Say instead:

✔ “Let’s confirm the measurements.”

✔ “Once we measure it, it’s clear.”

✔ “Size will tell us.”

Tip 9

Phrases That Kill Trust After the Sale

(Used when issues arise)

❌ “No one else has complained.”

❌ “That’s never happened before.”

❌ “It must’ve been installed wrong.”

Why to avoid:

They escalate frustration immediately.

Say instead:

✔ “Let’s work through what happened.”

✔ “We’ll figure out why it failed.”

✔ “Let’s check the application again.”

Tip 10

The One Phrase to Never Use

(Under any circumstances)

❌ “That’s the only one we have.”

Why:

It forces the customer into a bad decision.

Say instead:

✔ “This is what we stock — let’s make sure it’s suitable.”

✔ “We can look at alternatives if needed.”
