Tip 1

Silent Internal Triggers (No Customer Involvement)

Core Principle (Teach This First)

You don’t escalate because you’re slow — you escalate because accuracy matters.

The customer never needs to know why assistance is needed — only that it improves the outcome.

A trainee should automatically seek assistance if any one of the following is true:

The part is not dimensionally standard

The application involves load, speed, or safety

The customer says “this keeps failing”

The part has attachments, modifications, or damage

The trainee is choosing between two similar options

The part will be hard to return once installed

These are non-negotiable escalation points.

Tip 2

External Red Flags from the Customer

Seek assistance immediately if the customer says:

“It just needs to get me through”

“This is what they gave me last time”

“I don’t have the old one”

“I can make it work”

“It’s close enough”

These phrases usually indicate risk, not urgency.

Tip 3

What NOT to Ask the Customer

❌ “Are you in a hurry?”

❌ “Do you need this quickly?”

❌ “Can you wait while I check?”

❌ “Is it okay if I ask someone?”

Why:

Puts pressure back on the trainee

Invites rushed decisions

Makes accuracy optional

Tip 4

What to SAY Instead (Confidence-Preserving)

Use process-based statements — not questions.

“I’m just going to confirm this before we proceed.”

“I’ll get this checked so we don’t miss anything.”

“This is worth a quick second look.”

“I’m going to loop someone in to make sure this is right.”

These assume cooperation without requesting permission.

Tip 5

The 10-Second Escalation Script (Teach This)

When stepping away:

“I’m going to confirm this detail — it’ll save you time later.”

That’s it.

No apology. No explanation.

Tip 6

How a Supervisor Should Step In (Training Alignment)

To support trainees, supervisors should mirror the same language:

“Good call checking this.”

“This is one you don’t rush.”

“Let’s confirm the application.”

This reinforces that escalation = professionalism, not weakness.

Tip 7

Internal Rule of Thumb (Easy to Remember)

If you wouldn’t bet your own time or money on it — escalate.

This removes ego and speeds up good decisions.

Tip 8

Confidence Reset If the Customer Pushes

If the customer says:

“I’m in a hurry.”

Respond with:

“Understood — accuracy is what gets you out the door once.”

“This is the fastest way to avoid a redo.”

“Getting it right is quicker than replacing it.”

Tip 9

Final Trainee Checklist (Before Proceeding Alone)

Proceed without assistance only if:

✔ Part is clearly identified

✔ Measurements are confirmed

✔ Application is low risk

✔ No ambiguity exists

If any box is unchecked → escalate.
